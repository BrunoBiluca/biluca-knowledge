# Phrasal verbs

Phrasal verbs are combinations of a verb and one or more particles (prepositions, adverbs) **that together create a new meaning**.

> [!quote]- (Link) - [Phrasal Verbs Dictionary](https://www.englishpage.com/prepositions/phrasaldictionary.html)
> Dicionário muito completo de phrasal verbs.

Examples

1. The fear of failure can sometimes **hold back** people from pursuing their dreams.
2. My team and I need to **set up** a meeting with the client to discuss the project details,
3. It’s important to **break up** your tasks into smaller manageable parts to stay organized.
4. Don’t doubt your true potential. **Take on** new challenges and opportunities.
5. The research team will **carry out** a study to investigate the effects of the new drug.
6. Poor communication can **lead to** misunderstandings and conflicts.
7. I’m trying to **save up** some money to go on vacation next year.
8. The storm caused a power outage, **cut off** our internet connection.
