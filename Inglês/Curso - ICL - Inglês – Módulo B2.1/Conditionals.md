# Conditionals

We have basic 3 types of conditionals in English;

- **Zero conditionals** are cases to express when a condition is attended its consequence always happen
- **One conditionals** are cases to express when a condition is attended its consequence has a lot of probability of happen
- **Second conditionals** are cases to express imaginary conditionals or possibilities less likely to happen

Zero, First and Second conditionals in contrast

| Type of conditional | Structure                                | Example                                                                                                     |
| ------------------- | ---------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Zero conditional    | IF + present simple, present simple      | **If I set** the thermostat, the system **adjusts** the temperature                                         |
| One conditional     | IF + present simple, will + infinitive   | **If you get** a smart home device , you **will be able** to turn off all your lights with just one command |
| Second conditional  | IF + past simple, would/could infinitive | **If I had** a smart home, I **could control** everything from my phone.                                    |

Examples

- Zero conditional

1. If the system detects a leak or other potential water damage, it sends me an immediate notification so I can take action right away.

- One conditional

1. If my smart home were connected to my car, I could have the garage door open automatically as I approach, and have the lights turn on inside.

- Second conditional

1. If I ever forgot to lock the front door or turn off the stove, my smart home would send me a notification and allow me to take care of it no matter where I was.
